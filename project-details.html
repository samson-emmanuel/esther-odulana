Problem Statement

expand_more

The company faced a significant increase in customer churn, impacting revenue and growth. The primary objective was to move from a reactive to a proactive retention strategy by developing a predictive model to identify customers at high risk of churning, allowing for targeted intervention.

Methodology

expand_more

The project involved data collection from multiple sources, exploratory data analysis to identify key churn indicators, feature engineering, and training several classification models. A Logistic Regression model was chosen for its interpretability and performance, achieving high accuracy in predicting churn within a 30-day window.

Results & Impact

expand_more

The predictive model was integrated into the company's CRM, enabling the customer success team to engage with at-risk customers proactively. This led to a 12% reduction in monthly churn within the first quarter of implementation and provided a data-driven framework for future retention strategies.

Key Learnings

expand_more

A key insight was the importance of customer service interaction frequency as a predictor of churn. The project highlighted the value of cross-departmental data integration and established a repeatable MLOps pipeline for future predictive analytics projects.
