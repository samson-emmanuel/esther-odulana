Problem Statement & Business Goal

High customer churn rates were impacting revenue stability and growth. The primary business goal was to develop a predictive model that could identify customers likely to churn with at least 80% accuracy. This would enable the marketing team to launch targeted retention campaigns, reduce acquisition costs, and improve customer lifetime value.

The Process

Data Sourcing & Exploration

The analysis used a dataset of 10,000 anonymized customer records. Initial exploratory data analysis (EDA) revealed strong correlations between churn and factors like contract type (month-to-month), tenure, and monthly charges. Visualizations helped identify key segments of at-risk customers, guiding the feature engineering process.

Feature Engineering

Several new features were created to improve model performance. This included creating interaction terms (e.g., tenure * monthly charges) and binning numerical data into categorical groups. Categorical features were one-hot encoded, and all numerical features were scaled to ensure the model treated them equally.

Model Selection

Three classification models were tested: Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and XGBoost. After hyperparameter tuning using GridSearchCV, the XGBoost model demonstrated the best performance across accuracy, precision, and recall metrics, and was selected for the final implementation.

Business Impact & Recommendations

The model's predictions provide a clear, data-driven path to proactive customer retention. Key recommendations include: 1) Targeting customers in the top 20% of churn probability with personalized retention offers. 2) Developing loyalty programs for long-tenured customers who show early signs of churn risk. 3) Revisiting the month-to-month contract structure to encourage longer-term commitments.
